The primary value prop for ready should center on the tension between AI-accelerated velocity and engineering discipline.

Key insight: In the age of AI coding tools (Copilot, Claude Code, Cursor, etc.), teams can generate and ship code faster than ever — but velocity now consistently outruns discipline. The gap between "can ship" and "should ship" is wider than it's ever been.

Real-world evidence the user cited:
- Claude Code source leak
- Tech company data breaches
- Security and product breakdowns at otherwise-strong engineering orgs

**Why:** These aren't competence failures — they're discipline failures caused by velocity. Smart teams move fast, skip the prep work, and find out later that the scaffolding wasn't there.

**How to apply:** Lead with this framing in the README, landing page copy, and any pitch materials. `ready` isn't a bureaucratic checklist — it's the automated discipline layer that keeps pace with AI velocity. The faster your team ships, the more you need it. Frame the Before/After not as "manual vs automated" but as "discipline that can't keep up with velocity vs discipline that scales with it."

Specific copy direction:
- "AI tools made shipping faster. They didn't make shipping safer."
- The problem isn't that teams are careless — it's that the old discipline mechanisms (review meetings, manual checklists) don't scale to the speed AI enables
- ready is what lets you have both: AI velocity AND engineering discipline
