# Talking Points

The spine. Every presentation variant (3-min, 6-min, 15-min) hangs off these. If you only remember three sentences on stage, remember the pillars.

## The Three Pillars

1. **"AI made your team 10x faster at writing code. It did not make you 10x faster at being safe."**
   The villain. This is the opening line. Let it sit.

2. **"Readiness is the only part of shipping software that still runs at human speed."**
   The gap. Velocity is AI-speed, discipline is person-speed, and the delta is where incidents live.

3. **"What if review criteria lived in your repo, ran on every PR, and expired on their own?"**
   The reveal. Don't say the product name yet. Make them want it before you name it.

## Supporting Beats

Pick two or three — never all of them. They're seasoning, not the meal.

- "Every incident retro ends with the same sentence: *we should have caught this before launch*. Nobody ever builds the thing that would have caught it. Until now."
- "SonarQube checks your code. OPA checks your infra. Drata checks your regulators. **Nothing checks whether your service is actually *ready*.**"
- "Eighty-six checkpoints. Real services. Real incidents. Not a weekend experiment."
- "No server. No SaaS. No account. A folder, a scanner, a CI template. **Adoption is a pull request.**"
- "The discipline layer finally runs at the speed of the rest of your pipeline."

## The Categories You Are Not

When someone in the room asks "how is this different from X," you need these answers in your pocket:

| They ask about | You answer |
|----------------|------------|
| SonarQube / CodeQL | "Those check code quality. This checks whether your service meets its review requirements." |
| OPA / Sentinel | "Those enforce infra policy at deploy time. This enforces operational readiness across code *and* the stuff outside the repo." |
| Drata / Vanta / RegScale | "Those automate regulators. This automates **your own team's internal review standards** — the ones that currently live in a Google Doc." |
| Copilot / Cursor / Claude | "This is the discipline layer that keeps pace with them. It's the complement, not a competitor." |

## Words to Avoid

- "Compliance" — sounds like an auditor's tool. You're not selling to auditors.
- "Platform" — bloated, means nothing. You're selling a scanner.
- "AI-powered" — you are *for* AI-powered teams, you are not an AI product.
- "Revolutionary" / "groundbreaking" — let the demo say it.
- "Just" — "it just runs a scan." Never diminish your own product.

## Words That Land

- "Drift." Everyone has it. Nobody measures it.
- "Closed loop." Implies something was open before.
- "Ready." The product name is the verb. Use it as a verb when you can: "is your service ready?"
- "Discipline." Not compliance. Discipline is voluntary and respected. Compliance is imposed and resented.
- "Prep work." The thing you're replacing. Everyone knows what prep work is and everyone hates doing it.

## The Closing Line (pick one)

- "The discipline layer finally runs at the speed of the rest of your pipeline."
- "Every incident retro has the same sentence. Now you have the thing that would have caught it."
- "No infrastructure. No SaaS. No subscription. **It's on GitHub today.**"
